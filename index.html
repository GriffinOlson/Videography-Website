About Me

Ever since I discovered iMovie on my mom’s phone back in 2016, I haven’t wanted to stop making movies. Every chance I got, whether for a school project or just hanging out with my friends, I’d make a video. In 7th grade, my documentary about 20th century reformer Jacob Riis won a contest and got the school library named in his honor.

Now that I’m in college, I’ve shifted to music videos, as they are in demand, but have recently gained an interest in branded short films. They act as advertisements, but also as creative art in their own right, making them stand out from dry corporate commercials. The brand’s size, market, or products don’t matter to me; there is a fun ad concept to be found in any business.

When is an independent artist...

Right for your advertisement

  • You want to create personal ads specifically for your consumers.
  • You want the option to work closely with the artist without having to deal with an ad agency's corporate bureaucracy.
  • You just want to get the story of your business across.

Wrong for your advertisement

  • You have a massive marketing budget to spend on a top-of-the-line production company.
  • You value celebrity endorsements and cheap marketing stunts.
  • You want your advertisements to be bland enough to appease a mass audience.
